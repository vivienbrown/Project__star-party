Star Parties

Hang out and enjoy the night sky in a group setting. We will identify constellations, stars, asterisms, watch for meteors, observe the phases of the moon and learn from each other.

What is it?

I am holding star parties each week, weather permitting. Join me in going out to the nature park and observing the sky!

The Rules

  • Please take good care of yourself. Bring your own snacks, water, thermos, etc
  • Take care of each other. If somebody shares a cup of hot tea, offer something in return if you can, but do not begrudge anyone who cannot share for any reason
  • Be responsible for your own things. If you bring equipment, please keep track of it, set it up and put it away. You are solely responsible for your own things.
  • Respect other people's things. If someone has set up a time lapse of the sky try not to get in front of it. If someone offers you a look through their binoculars or telescope, do not adjust their settings. If you are holding something small of someone elses or borrowed something during the night, when it comes time to return it please hand it back to them in their hand, do not leave it near them or with their things. Make it easy for others to keep track of their things.
  • Conversations are welcome, as is music, but please keep the volume down.
  • For night vision preservation, please keep lights to a minimum. Be mindful of flashlights, phone screens, vehicle headlights and taillights can all interfere with night vision and it can take 20 minutes to an hour to restore.
  • Please respect your surroundings and other people by cleaning up after yourself. Before leaving, do a quick scan of the area you occupied and make sure you haven't forgotten anything or left any trash.
  • Wherever possible, give as much as you take.
